Date         : April 2026
Data files   : game_records.csv, ratings.csv
Study scope  : 4 agents, 40 seeds per matchup, 240 total games

Agents included:
  - Heuristic
  - MCTS-All
  - MCTS-Vanilla
  - Random

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This study contains 240 head-to-head games:
  - 6 pairwise matchups
  - 40 seeds per matchup
  - 2 agents per game

The main result is that MCTS-All is the strongest overall agent in this study.
It has the highest aggregate win rate across all opponents and ranks first by
conservative TrueSkill, while Heuristic ranks first by ELO. Heuristic is the
second-strongest agent and remains competitive with MCTS-All in direct play.
MCTS-Vanilla is clearly weaker than both MCTS-All and Heuristic, while Random
is not competitive.

No draws occurred in this dataset.

4. HEAD-TO-HEAD MATCHUPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  Heuristic vs MCTS-All
--------------------------

Record:
  - Heuristic: 19 wins
  - MCTS-All: 21 wins
  - Win rate for Heuristic: 47.5%
  - Win rate for MCTS-All: 52.5%

Other matchup statistics:
  - Average game length: 161.18 turns
  - Games reaching the 200-turn cap: 18 / 40
  - Average final length:
      Heuristic  = 20.75
      MCTS-All   = 21.52

Interpretation:
  This is the closest matchup in the study. MCTS-All has a narrow edge, but it
  is small. The long average game length and large number of capped games show
  that these agents are closely matched and often survive deep into the game.
  In practical terms, the study supports the claim that MCTS-All is at least as
  strong as Heuristic, but not overwhelmingly stronger.

Approximate 95% confidence interval for Heuristic win rate:
  32.0% to 63.0%

That interval includes 50%, so this matchup alone does not justify a strong
claim that one agent decisively dominates the other.

4.2  Heuristic vs MCTS-Vanilla
------------------------------

Record:
  - Heuristic: 25 wins
  - MCTS-Vanilla: 15 wins
  - Win rate for Heuristic: 62.5%

Other matchup statistics:
  - Average game length: 165.43 turns
  - Games reaching the 200-turn cap: 21 / 40
  - Average final length:
      Heuristic     = 21.93
      MCTS-Vanilla  = 20.00

Interpretation:
  Heuristic has a meaningful edge over MCTS-Vanilla. The result suggests that
  the heuristic policy is already strong enough to outperform unguided or less
  refined search. This is consistent with the code design: the heuristic agent
  uses a fairly rich evaluation pipeline, while MCTS-Vanilla lacks priors,
  opponent-aware root scoring, heuristic rollouts, and UCB1-Tuned.

Approximate 95% confidence interval for Heuristic win rate:
  47.5% to 77.5%

This still leaves uncertainty, but the central estimate clearly favours
Heuristic.

4.3  MCTS-Vanilla vs MCTS-All
-----------------------------

Record:
  - MCTS-Vanilla: 12 wins
  - MCTS-All: 28 wins
  - Win rate for MCTS-Vanilla: 30.0%
  - Win rate for MCTS-All: 70.0%

Other matchup statistics:
  - Average game length: 157.90 turns
  - Games reaching the 200-turn cap: 21 / 40
  - Average final length:
      MCTS-Vanilla  = 17.90
      MCTS-All      = 21.90

Interpretation:
  This is the clearest evidence in the dataset that the added MCTS guidance is
  valuable. MCTS-All substantially outperforms MCTS-Vanilla, which indicates
  that the extra machinery in the improved configuration is not just cosmetic.
  The strongest likely contributors are the root priors, heuristic rollouts,
  and improved selection policy.

Approximate 95% confidence interval for MCTS-Vanilla win rate:
  15.8% to 44.2%

Since the interval lies well below 50%, this matchup gives the strongest
support for the superiority of MCTS-All in the study.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WHAT THE RESULTS SAY ABOUT THE AGENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1  MCTS-All
-------------

MCTS-All is the best overall performer in the study by aggregate win rate,
average placement, and conservative TrueSkill. Its direct edge over Heuristic
is narrow, but its edge over MCTS-Vanilla is large. This supports the view
that the improved search configuration adds real value beyond plain MCTS.

The most defensible conclusion is:
  MCTS-All is the strongest agent in this dataset overall, even though its
  direct advantage over Heuristic is small rather than decisive.

5.2  Heuristic
--------------

Heuristic remains a very strong baseline. It comfortably defeats MCTS-Vanilla
and is nearly even with MCTS-All. This is important because it shows that a
well-designed evaluation function plus one-step lookahead is highly effective
in this environment.

The heuristic agent is not just a baseline to beat; it is a genuinely strong
competitor in its own right.

5.3  MCTS-Vanilla
-----------------

MCTS-Vanilla is clearly better than Random, but it is not competitive with the
best two agents. Its weaker results against both Heuristic and MCTS-All imply
that search alone is not enough here; guidance matters. In this codebase, the
improved MCTS configuration appears to depend heavily on heuristic structure.

5.4  Random
-----------

Random validates the floor of the experiment. It almost never wins, survives
for fewer turns, and finishes with much smaller final length. This confirms
that the study is sensitive enough to distinguish intelligent agents from a
non-strategic baseline.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. EFFECT OF LONG GAMES AND TURN CAPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Out of 240 games, 60 reached the 200-turn cap:

  60 / 240 = 25.0%

This matters for interpretation:
  - Many games between the stronger agents are effectively decided by end-state
    length rather than early elimination.
  - That makes final length an important supporting metric, not just survival.
  - It also explains why the strongest matchups have very long average game
    lengths, especially:
      Heuristic vs MCTS-All
      Heuristic vs MCTS-Vanilla
      MCTS-Vanilla vs MCTS-All

Across all games:
  - Average game length: 106.18 turns
  - Median game length: 102.5 turns
  - Average non-capped game length: 74.91 turns

The stronger matchups are much longer than the global average because games
against Random end quickly and pull the overall mean downward.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. MOST DEFENSIBLE CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The strongest claims supported by this 40-seed study are:

  1. MCTS-All and Heuristic form the top tier.

  2. MCTS-All is the strongest overall agent in the full dataset.
     This claim is supported by aggregate win rate, average placement, and
     conservative TrueSkill.

  3. Heuristic is competitive with MCTS-All.
     The head-to-head result is close enough that it should be described as a
     narrow MCTS-All edge, not a decisive separation.

  4. MCTS-All clearly outperforms MCTS-Vanilla.
     This is the cleanest direct evidence that the improved MCTS design helps.

  5. Random is an appropriate floor baseline and is dramatically weaker than
     all other agents.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. LIMITATIONS OF THIS RESULTS SET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a much stronger dataset than a 3-seed pilot, but it still has limits:

  - The top matchup is close.
    Heuristic vs MCTS-All does not provide overwhelming separation.

  - Some confidence intervals remain wide.
    Forty seeds is enough for useful conclusions, but not enough to eliminate
    all uncertainty in close matchups.

  - Many games reach the turn cap.
    This makes end-game length management important and can compress the gap
    between strong agents.

  - These are local headless results.
    They are highly useful for controlled comparison, but they should still be
    described as results from this simulator setup.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. FINAL SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The 40-seed small study shows a clear overall ranking:

  1. MCTS-All
  2. Heuristic
  3. MCTS-Vanilla
  4. Random

The key qualitative takeaway is not simply that "MCTS beats heuristic."
A more accurate conclusion is:

  The fully improved MCTS agent is the best overall performer, but the
  heuristic baseline remains very strong and runs it close in direct play.
  The largest and clearest performance jump is between MCTS-Vanilla and
  MCTS-All, which indicates that the added MCTS guidance mechanisms are
  materially useful in this codebase.
